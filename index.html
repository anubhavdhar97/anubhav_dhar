About Me

I am a PhD student in Dr. Saravanan Palani's lab (Synthetic Cell Biology Lab) at the Department of Biochemistry at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore. My research is primarily focused on cell biology of the cytoskeleton, specifically investigating actin and associated proteins like tropomyosin.

A key aspect of my work involves developing and optimizing novel probes that enable cutting-edge advanced microscopy techniques, such as Expansion Microscopy (ExM), to visualize cellular structures at super-resolution. I am dedicated to pushing the boundaries of cellular imaging and dissecting molecular mechanisms.

My professional interests lie in understanding the fundamental principles of how cytsokeletal networks define cell organization and division, with a strong emphasis on molecular mechanisms and tool development for microscopy based-research. My skills include advanced microscopy, molecular biology, biochemical analysis, and rigorous experimental design and analysis.
